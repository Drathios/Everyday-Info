How Mango Enhances the Effects of Marijuana

Photo courtesy of Sensei Seeds

When consuming marijuana, many users seek to enhance their experience in various ways. One such method involves the consumption of mangoes. It may sound surprising, but the humble mango has a compound that can potentially intensify and prolong the high from marijuana. Here’s a closer look at why this happens and how it works.

The Science Behind It

The primary reason mangoes can enhance the effects of marijuana lies in a compound called myrcene. Myrcene is a type of terpene, which are aromatic compounds found in many plants, including cannabis. These terpenes play a crucial role in the unique flavors and effects of different cannabis strains.

1. Myrcene's Role in Absorption and Effect

Myrcene has the ability to increase the permeability of cell membranes, including those in the brain. This increased permeability allows cannabinoids like THC (tetrahydrocannabinol), the psychoactive compound in marijuana, to be absorbed more efficiently and quickly. As a result, users might experience a stronger and longer-lasting high.

2. Synergistic Interaction

Terpenes such as myrcene can interact synergistically with cannabinoids. This means that when consumed together, the effects of THC and myrcene might be amplified, leading to a more intense experience than if either compound were consumed alone.

3. Relaxation and Sedation

Myrcene is known for its sedative and relaxing properties. When combined with the effects of THC, myrcene can contribute to a more relaxed and prolonged high, making the overall experience more enjoyable for some users.

How to Use Mangoes with Marijuana

To potentially enhance your marijuana experience with mangoes, follow these steps:

1. Choose Ripe Mangoes

Ripe mangoes have higher concentrations of myrcene. Look for mangoes that are soft to the touch and emit a sweet fragrance.

2. Timing is Key

Consume the mango about 45 minutes to an hour before using marijuana. This allows enough time for the myrcene to be absorbed into your bloodstream.

3. Quantity

Eating one or two mangoes should suffice. However, individual responses can vary, so it may take some experimentation to find the right amount for you.

Precautions

While combining mangoes and marijuana can enhance the experience for some, it’s important to approach this method with caution. Individual reactions to both substances can vary, and consuming too much can lead to discomfort or an overwhelming high. Start with a small amount and see how your body responds before making any adjustments.
